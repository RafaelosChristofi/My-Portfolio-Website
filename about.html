Rafaelos Christofi was born in Nicosia, the capital of the small island of Cyprus. He is an acoustic and electroacoustic composer, film scorer and sound designer. He studied classical guitar at the Music School of Nicosia, Classical Composition at Codarts University of Arts and Composition for Media at Katarina Gurska Centro Superior. He wrote music for short films, documentaries, opera, theatre, and many other interdisciplinary settings. He collaborated and wrote music for various artists, music groups and organisations, including the Dutch National Opera and Ballet and the International Film Festival of Rotterdam. His Cypriot roots are apperent in his music and many of his works are also deeply programatic, as they are written for and inspired by stories and tales, achieving a deeper connection between fantasy, story, stage, expectation and sound. Good examples would be the pieces Myst (2017), More than the Sum of its Parts (2022), Tales of the Forest (2023) and El joven Capitán de la Sirena (2023). Rafaelos was awarded the first prize for the International Youth Music Competition in 2021, the first prize for the Cyprus National Commission UNESCO for a short documentary in 2021 and was nominated for "Best Original Music" at the IRTVE Premios 2024.
